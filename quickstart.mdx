# Quickstart Guide

This guide will walk you through setting up your Relio account and creating your first contact, property, and deal. Let's get started!

<Note>
This guide assumes you're using the web application. The mobile and desktop apps have similar workflows with native UI adaptations.
</Note>

## Prerequisites

- Access to the Relio web application (hosted or self-hosted)
- A valid email address for account creation

## Step 1: Create Your Account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign Up">
    Navigate to the Relio web application and click **Sign Up**.
    
    Enter your:
    - Full name
    - Email address
    - Password (minimum requirements will be enforced)
    
    Click **Create Account** to continue.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify Your Email">
    Check your inbox for a verification email from Relio.
    
    Click the verification link to confirm your email address.
    
    <Tip>
    Can't find the email? Check your spam folder or click "Resend verification email" on the login page.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Sign In">
    Return to the login page and sign in with your email and password.
    
    Relio uses **Better Auth** for secure authentication with session management.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 2: Create Your Workspace

<Steps>
  <Step title="Workspace Setup">
    On first login, you'll be prompted to create your first workspace (organization).
    
    Enter your:
    - **Workspace Name** - Your company or team name (e.g., "Smith Realty Group")
    - **Workspace Slug** - A URL-friendly identifier (e.g., "smith-realty")
    
    <Info>
    The workspace slug will be part of your Relio URL: `app.relio.com/smith-realty`
    
    Workspace names are checked for profanity and must be unique.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Your Plan">
    Select your billing plan:
    - **Free** - 1 seat, basic features
    - **Pro** - Multiple seats, advanced features, API access
    
    You'll start with a free trial of Pro features to explore Relio's full capabilities.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Default Pipeline Created">
    Relio automatically creates a default CRE (Commercial Real Estate) deal pipeline with stages:
    
    1. Canvassing
    2. Qualification  
    3. LOI (Letter of Intent)
    4. Due Diligence
    5. Closed Won
    6. Closed Lost
    
    You can customize this pipeline later or create additional pipelines for different deal types.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 3: Add Your First Contact

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Contacts">
    From the main navigation, click **Contacts** to open the contacts page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Contact">
    Click the **+ New Contact** button in the top right.
    
    Fill in the contact details:
    ```json
    {
      "firstName": "John",
      "lastName": "Smith",
      "email": [{
        "address": "john.smith@example.com",
        "isPrimary": true
      }],
      "phone": [{
        "number": "+1-555-0100",
        "label": "Mobile",
        "isPrimary": true
      }],
      "role": "buyer",
      "stage": "lead"
    }
    ```
    
    <Info>
    Contacts support multiple emails, phone numbers, and addresses. Mark one as primary for quick reference.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Contact Details">
    Enhance your contact record with:
    - **Title** - Job title or role
    - **Description** - Notes about the contact
    - **Social Links** - LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram
    - **Buyer Needs** - Property preferences (type, price range, units, etc.)
    - **Connection Strength** - How well you know them (weak/medium/strong)
    
    Click **Save** to create the contact.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View Contact Details">
    Click on your newly created contact to open the detail page.
    
    Explore the tabs:
    - **Overview** - All contact information with inline editing
    - **Activity** - Timeline of notes, calls, emails, and tasks
    - **Related** - Linked companies, properties, and deals
    - **Files** - Document attachments
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 4: Create a Property Record

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Properties">
    Click **Properties** in the main navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Property">
    Click **+ New Property** to open the property creation dialog.
    
    Enter the property address:
    ```
    123 Main Street, San Francisco, CA 94102
    ```
    
    Relio will automatically geocode the address and populate:
    - Latitude/longitude
    - City, state, zip
    - County information
    
    <Tip>
    Use the address autocomplete feature for accurate geocoding. Relio integrates with mapping services for precise location data.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Property Details">
    Fill in property information:
    - **Property Type** - Residential, commercial, land, industrial, multi-family
    - **Status** - Active, pending, sold, off-market
    - **List Price** - Current asking price (stored in cents internally)
    - **Bedrooms/Bathrooms** - For residential properties
    - **Square Footage** - Building and lot size
    - **Year Built** - Construction year
    
    Advanced fields:
    - **MLS ID** - Multiple Listing Service identifier
    - **APN/Parcel ID** - County assessor parcel number
    - **Zoning** - Property zoning designation
    - **1031 Exchange** - Yes/No for exchange eligibility
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View on Map">
    Switch to the **Map** view to see your property visualized.
    
    If available, parcel boundaries will be displayed from county assessor data.
    
    <Info>
    Relio uses PostGIS for geospatial queries and Mapbox for map rendering. Property locations are stored as `geometry(Point, 4326)` in the database.
    </Info>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 5: Create Your First Deal

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Deals">
    Click **Deals** in the main navigation to open the deal pipeline view.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Deal">
    Click **+ New Deal** to create a deal.
    
    Enter deal information:
    - **Deal Name** - Descriptive name (e.g., "123 Main St Purchase")
    - **Emoji** - Optional emoji icon for quick visual identification
    - **Value** - Deal amount in dollars (stored as cents)
    - **Probability** - Likelihood of closing (0-100%)
    - **Expected Close Date** - Target closing date
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Link Records">
    Connect related records to your deal:
    - **Contact** - Select the buyer/seller (John Smith from Step 3)
    - **Property** - Link the property (123 Main St from Step 4)
    - **Company** - Optionally link a company
    
    <Info>
    Linking records creates a relationship graph. View all deals for a contact from their detail page, or see all contacts interested in a property.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Stage & Pipeline">
    Choose your deal stage:
    - **Pipeline** - Default CRE Pipeline (or custom pipeline)
    - **Stage** - Canvassing, Qualification, LOI, Due Diligence, etc.
    
    The `stageEnteredAt` timestamp is automatically tracked when you change stages.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Visibility">
    Set deal sharing preferences:
    - **Private** - Only visible to you
    - **Workspace** - Visible to entire workspace with configurable access level
    - **Selected Users** - Share with specific team members
    
    Access levels:
    - **View** - Read-only access
    - **Edit** - Can modify deal details
    - **Full Access** - Can edit and delete
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 6: Add an Activity

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Contact or Deal">
    Navigate back to your contact or deal detail page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Activity">
    In the **Activity** tab, you'll see the activity editor.
    
    Create a note or log a call:
    - Click the **Note** or **Call** button
    - Type your content in the rich text editor (powered by TipTap)
    - Optionally @mention team members
    - Click **Save** to add the activity
    
    <Tip>
    Activities are shown in reverse chronological order. System activities (record created, field updated) are automatically tracked.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Real-Time Collaboration">
    If other users are viewing the same record, you'll see:
    - **Avatar stack** at the top showing who's currently viewing
    - **Typing indicators** when someone is composing a note
    - **Live updates** when activities are added without manual refresh
    
    <Info>
    Relio uses Supabase Realtime Presence for real-time collaboration features. Each workspace has a presence channel: `workspace:{orgSlug}`
    </Info>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Mobile & Desktop Apps

Once you're comfortable with the web app, download Relio for your other devices:

- **iOS/Android** - Search for "Relio CRM" in the App Store or Google Play
- **Desktop** - Download the native app for Windows, Mac, or Linux from the downloads page

All platforms sync in real-time, so your data is always up to date.

<Warning>
Make sure to complete email verification before using mobile or desktop apps. Authentication is handled by Better Auth across all platforms.
</Warning>
